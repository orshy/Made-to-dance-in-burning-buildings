Made to Dance in Burning Buildings

One woman's journey to make a difference in the fight against rape culture in our community.

Six hundred people are raped every day in the United States, 1 person every 2 minutes. There are 157 million women in the United States and 1 in 3 of them is a survivor of sexual assault or rape. Like most survivors of rape, women are at an increased risk for developing depression, PTSD, and addiction. PTSD affects hundred of thousands of people who have been exposed to violent events such as rape. Sometimes survivors become stuck in problem behaviors when their pain is not acknowledged, heard, respected, or understood.

Because of the pervasive nature of rape culture, the victim is often blamed for the assault. This leads many women to not report their rapes. Women choose to “suffer in silence” rather than seek help to protect themselves from victim-blaming and the humiliation they face when they do come forward. Only 1 in 6 reported cases ever see the inside of a courtroom. 97% of rapists never spend a day in jail.

Shot on location in Portland, Oregon and New York City, Forever Bruised But Not Broken dives into the issue of multi-cultural women and sexual assault. It asks why this is happening and why so little is being done to help these women. It profiles the lives of four survivors of diverse backgrounds and their experiences with PTSD. And it chronicles one women's journey to make a difference in the fight against rape culture in our community.

The film tells the story of Anya Pearson who is a survivor. An accomplished actress and writer, she’s written a play about women and rape which she is to bringing to the stage, to empower other survivors. This is her journey. The journey of the play, from page to stage. Anya's passionate quest to effect real meaningful social change through the arts. Can she make a difference in the lives of other survivors with her play? Can she change the world through the power of theater? Can she heal herself by healing others?

Synopsis

Made to Dance in Burning Buildings is a choreopoem, which fuses poetry, music, and violent and visceral contemporary dance posing the question: how do we heal from trauma? It's about a young black woman who is raped by her boyfriend's friends while he watches and develops PTSD as a result. Metaphorically, she fractures into six different women who each represent a different facet of her personality (Compassion, Despair, Madness, Romance, Missing You, and Perseverance) and these women poetically narrate her journey through PTSD, while she and the men from her real life, her first love and her abusers, explore through dance, the trauma and what led up to it.

The Team

Anya Pearson

Actress/Playwright/Screenwriter/Poet/
Producer/activist

Anya is the inaugural winner of the $10,000 Voice is a Muscle Grant from the Corporeal Voices Foundation. She is the author of the choreopoem, Made to Dance in Burning Buildings, which is a fusion of poetry, music, and violent and visceral contemporary dance which poses the question: how do we heal from trauma? This play, and Anya's passionate quest to see it through to full production in order to empower other survivors, is a key feature of the documentary. Anya is producing a workshop of the play in February, which will be filmed for the documentary. She will produce the workshop as well as star in it. She is the author of several other plays and screenplays.

As an actress, she has appeared in numerous regional theater productions as well as independent films. She is a proud member of Actors' Equity Association. Her poems have been published in the literary journals Elohi Gadugi and M Review. Her poem, "The Accomplice" was also featured in the BaseRoots Theatre Company main stage show, My Soul Grown Deep: 300 Years of African-American Poetry from Phillis Wheatley to Saul Williams and Anya Pearson, which she also co-produced. She is a graduate of the prestigious two-year program at the William Esper Studio in New York City where she studied acting with Terry Knickerbocker. She is also a graduate of the writing program at Marylhurst University. From 2009-2013, she worked as Operations Manager for BaseRoots Theatre Company.

Jerry Bell

Producer/Director/Actor

Jerry Bell Jr is an award winning film Director, an award winning Film Producer and a tv/film Actor. An Atlanta, GA native, Jerry traveled most of his early years as a military child. He also served in the United State Air Force for 8 years. He is an educated business man who resigned from a long career at one of America's most prominent technology companies to feed his passion of entertaining through film. As an actor Jerry has performed in multiple national broadcast TV shows and commercials (Z Nation, Grimm, Leverage, The Librarians, Vonage, Powerade, to name a few). He is most noticed as the #swifferdad in the "Deep Couch Sitting" Swiffer commercial along with his little son Jerry Bell III. Jerry is the founder of JBJR Productions who's primary focus is creating films that address issues challenging our society.
